## Design philosophy

The Scythe is a deliberate **bicycle-of-drones**: a structural plywood
airframe that any furniture factory in Ukraine (or NATO partner state)
can produce. **30 fully-assembled units stack inside a single shipping
container**, simplifying logistics.

## Guidance modes

| Altitude | Method | EW resistance |
|---|---|---|
| 30 m (low) | **Laser altimeter** terrain-following | GPS jamming irrelevant |
| 3,000 m (high) | **Visual positioning** against roads/landmarks | Robust against GPS denial |
| Terminal | Optional manual video link | Costs more, shorter range |

## Swarming tactic

Terminal Autonomy publicly markets a **leader-follower swarm pattern**:
> "One lead drone carrying a navigation system is followed by nine basic
> drones, which help saturate the target area and draw off enemy
> defenses from the lethal version."

This is a textbook **saturation-economic** doctrine: force Russian
Pantsir-S1 / Tor / Buk batteries to spend $1M interceptors on $15k decoys
until magazines deplete.
