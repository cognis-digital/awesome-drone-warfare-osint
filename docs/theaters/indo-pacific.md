# Indo-Pacific — strategic UAV posture

> No active high-intensity drone war (yet), but the **PLA UAV arsenal**
> and the **US Replicator initiative** are the largest near-peer
> capability programs in the world.

## US response — Replicator initiative

The Pentagon's **Replicator Initiative** is explicitly Indo-Pacific–oriented:
> *"deploy uncrewed systems en masse, allowing the US military to disperse
>  combat power over a large number of relatively inexpensive systems"*
> — [CRS IF12611](https://www.congress.gov/crs-product/IF12611)

Replicator products announced 2024-2025:
- **Anduril Bolt-M** (strike), **Anduril Roadrunner-M** (interceptor)
- **AeroVironment Switchblade 600**
- **Saronic Spyglass / Cutlass** USVs
- **General Atomics Quantum-X**
Reference: [DefenseScoop](https://defensescoop.com/2025/09/03/dod-replicator-drone-tech-transition-fielding-questions-linger/) · [DefenseNews](https://www.defensenews.com/unmanned/2024/11/13/pentagon-announces-new-batch-of-drones-for-replicator-program/).

## Taiwan implications

A peer-on-peer Taiwan Strait scenario would feature:
* PLA Wing Loong + GJ-11 swarms vs. US/Taiwan C-UAS
* Saturated maritime UAS attack on USN expeditionary forces
* High-band EW environment (PLA's 88-band coverage)

Lessons from **Ukraine, Red Sea, and Sudan** are explicitly being mined
by [CSIS](https://www.csis.org/analysis/lessons-ukraine-conflict-modern-warfare-age-autonomy-information-and-resilience) and [Hudson](https://www.hudson.org/national-security-defense/the-fall-and-rise-of-russian-electronic-warfare) to shape Indo-Pacific posture.
