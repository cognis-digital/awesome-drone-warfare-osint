# 🇲🇲 Myanmar — 3D-printed insurgent drones

> Anti-junta resistance groups (People's Defence Force + Ethnic Armed
> Organizations) have stood up a **distributed 3D-printed drone industry**
> against the Tatmadaw's air dominance — a low-cost analogue of Ukraine's
> Wild Hornets model.

## Documented practice

* **Fuselages 3D-printed** then covered with fiberglass.
* Batteries, motors, control surfaces sourced from Chinese e-commerce
  platforms (AliExpress, Taobao re-exports).
* University students and engineers run the production cells.

Sources: [WIRED](https://www.wired.com/story/the-rebel-drone-maker-of-myanmar/), [GNET](https://gnet-research.org/2024/10/14/3d-printing-in-conflict-zones-a-game-changer/), [Jamestown Foundation](https://jamestown.org/anti-junta-rebels-resort-to-3d-printed-weapons-in-myanmar/).

## Why it matters for the dataset

Myanmar is the **canonical case of grassroots drone industrialization
under sanctions and embargo**. It validates the thesis that **3D printing
+ open-source autopilots + Chinese commodity electronics = sustainable
insurgent UAS capability**.

The Ukrainian model is the high-end analog; Myanmar is the proof-of-concept
that low-bandwidth, low-budget actors can replicate it.
