# 🇾🇪 Red Sea / Yemen — Houthi long-range strikes

> Since November 2023, the Houthi (Ansar Allah) movement has launched
> hundreds of UAV + cruise-missile + anti-ship missile sorties against
> commercial shipping and coalition warships in the Red Sea and Gulf of Aden.

| Platform | Class | Origin lineage |
|---|---|---|
| **Samad-1** (recon) / **Samad-2** / **Samad-3** | Loitering / strike | Iranian-derived |
| **Qasef-1**, **Qasef-2K** | Loitering munition | Variant of Iranian Ababil-T |
| **"Mersad"** | Loitering | Iranian-derived |
| **Waid** family | Long-range UAV | Iranian-supplied |
| **Shahed-136** copies | Loitering | Direct Iranian transfer |
| Commercial-derived ISR | Recon | Off-the-shelf DJI / Autel |

## Forensic findings (CAR + Orion Policy)

The [Conflict Armament Research dispatch](https://storymaps.arcgis.com/stories/46283842630243379f0504ece90a821f) and
[Orion Policy supply-chain study](https://orionpolicy.org/the-houthi-drone-supply-chain/) document that:

1. Houthi UAVs share **heat-shrink sleeving, connectors, and other commodity hardware** with IEDs documented in Yemen and Bahrain — implying a single procurement network.
2. **Electronic components are imported** from China and the broader gulf re-export network.
3. The **Samad-3 / Waid-2 share airframe lineage with Iranian Toufan-II / Shahed-136**.

## Significance

The Red Sea theater is the **first sustained anti-shipping drone campaign**
of the modern era. It demonstrates that Iranian/Shahed-class munitions
work against moving naval targets, not just static infrastructure —
shaping how navies (USN, RN, FN) plan for **layered C-UAS at sea**
(SeaRAM, ESSM, RAM, DragonFire laser, decoys).
