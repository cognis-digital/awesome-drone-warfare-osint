# 🇸🇩 Sudan civil war (April 2023 – present)

> Per ACLED, **1,000+ drone attacks** between April 2023 and February 2026.
> Both the **Rapid Support Forces (RSF)** and **Sudanese Armed Forces (SAF)**
> field foreign-supplied UAVs.

| Side | Documented platforms | Origin |
|---|---|---|
| **SAF** (army) | Bayraktar TB2, Akinci | 🇹🇷 Turkey |
| **RSF** (paramilitary) | CH-95, "smaller commercial-class" UAVs | 🇨🇳 China |
| **Both** | Repurposed DJI Mavic / Autel | 🇨🇳 (origin) |

## Casualty tally (ACLED-derived)

| Period | RSF-attributed drone deaths | SAF-attributed drone deaths |
|---|---|---|
| 2023–2025 | ~780 civilians | ~1,800 (per Pulitzer Center / AJ) |

Sources: [Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/longform/2026/2/3/drone-warfare-in-sudan-tracking-1000-aerial-attacks-since-april-2023), [Pulitzer Center](https://pulitzercenter.org/stories/drones-are-reshaping-sudans-civil-war), [Critical Threats](https://www.criticalthreats.org/analysis/drones-over-sudan-foreign-powers-in-sudans-civil-war), [Soufan Center](https://thesoufancenter.org/intelbrief-2025-october-24/), [Sudan Tribune](https://sudantribune.com/article/310795).

## Foreign-power dynamics

- **🇦🇪 UAE** allegedly resupplies RSF — UN Panel of Experts citations.
- **🇪🇬 Egypt** aligned with SAF (Bayraktar TB2 deliveries).
- **🇨🇳 China** (CASC) supplies CH-95 platforms.
- **🇺🇸 / 🇪🇺** sanctions on key brokers in 2024–2025.

This theater is significant because **drone proliferation reaches sub-Saharan
conflict without the high-intensity Russia/Ukraine cost-benefit math**;
casualties per drone are higher and attribution harder.
